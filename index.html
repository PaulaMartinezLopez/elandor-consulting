Profile

The CFO who also builds the system

Most finance functions have the strategy. What they lack is the infrastructure to see it clearly. I bridge that gap — as a CFO who understands the business deeply, and as someone who can build the reporting systems that make decisions faster and better-informed.

My background spans manufacturing, international trading, and cross-border operations — environments where financial complexity is real and visibility is critical. Over 15 years I have built finance functions from scratch, led ERP implementations, structured PE-grade reporting, and designed end-to-end dashboards that management actually uses.

I work with what exists. Whether a company has a full ERP, runs on Excel, or is building from zero — the approach adapts. The goal is always the same: reliable information, in the right format, for the people who need to act on it.

What I do

Finance systems that serve the business

Management Reporting & Dashboards
One version of the numbers. Always ready. No manual intervention.

A reporting system connected to your data — ERP or Excel — that gives management the information they need without asking the finance team to prepare it. Month-end stops being an event. Decisions stop waiting.
Operational Visibility
Finance across the whole business, not just the P&L.

Stock, margins, credit risk, production — visible in real time, not reconstructed at month-end. Sales knows who owes. Operations knows what's moving. Management knows where the cash is.
Financial Planning & Structure
A model that works when conditions change.

Rolling forecasts, scenario analysis, and budget structures built to be used — not filed. When something shifts, you run the numbers in hours, not days. And you see the P&L impact before you make the call.
